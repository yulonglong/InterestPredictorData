        Background

        Summary

        A public historian, published author, and information professional with a Master's Degree in History focusing on Public History and Archival Administration. Two decades of experience in the information services industry, specializing in special collections and archives, historical research, local archives and public records research, government and public policy research, and legislative history and bill tracking. Extensive experience with online research and social media networks and applications.

        Research interests include: Digital humanities, archives and records management, public history, Tennessee history, publishing, social media advocacy and outreach for cultural heritage organizations, open government, information policy, legislative history, and civic engagement.

        Experience

        Director of Public Services

        Tennessee State Library & Archives
        July 2012 – Present (3 years 1 month)

        As director, I lead the reference and research functions of the Tennessee State Library and Archives (TSLA), supervising professional and paraprofessional librarians and archivists, including staff of the library and manuscripts reading rooms, microfilm reading room, interlibrary loan, legislative history and research services.

        In addition to my duties as Director of Public Services, I also collaboratively manage TSLA's social media efforts, primarily focusing on developing content for the TSLA Blog, and providing research support for TSLA's Facebook and Flickr sites.

        The Tennessee State Library and Archives collects and preserves books and records of historical, documentary and reference value with an emphasis in Tennessee history, and promotes library and archival development throughout the state. The Public Services Section provides reference assistance and access to collections, including books, periodicals, documents, maps, photographs, music, and audiovisual materials.

        Author and Founding Editor

        The Posterity Project
        July 2008 – Present (7 years 1 month)

        I have held a lifelong passion for history, and as founding editor and publisher of The Posterity Project, I offer "reflections on archives, public history, and memory" from my home state of Tennessee. The Posterity Project is also an author platform, where I share information and excerpts about my writing projects. I am the author of 'John Sevier: Tennessee's First Hero,' published by The History Press, and I have devoted much of my writings on The Posterity Project to explore how Tennessee's first governor, John Sevier, is remembered in history and literature.

        Since the launch of The Posterity Project in 2008, I have been a vocal advocate of social media as a means to connect archivists and archival institutions with the public, particularly within my home state of Tennessee. In November 2009 I was honored to receive the Society of Tennessee Archivists' John H. Thweatt Archival Advancement Award for my work "to advance the archival profession in Tennessee and the Southeast" through social media and The Posterity Project. Since then, I have used The Posterity Project as a platform for archival advocacy, and have delivered presentations to local archives, genealogical and historical societies about using social media, and about how cultural heritage organizations can leverage social media in their outreach efforts.

        Library Manager

        First Amendment Center at Vanderbilt University
        August 2003 – July 2012 (9 years)

        Responsible for daily operation and administration of library activities including collection development, reference and research services. Monitored legislation and public policy affecting First Amendment freedoms. Developed curriculum and provided instruction to faculty and students for Internet and library research training using primary and secondary sources. Maintained and updated First Amendment Center Library digital catalogs and Intranet site. Interviewed, trained and supervised interns and support staff.

        Assisted in the creation and development of content for the First Amendment Center's social media sites, and wrote and edited content for the First Amendment Center website on legislative issues and history. Historical works include, “George Mason: Honoring a Forgotten Founder,” “Remembering the Father of the Constitution: James Madison and the First Amendment,” “Public access to presidential records: Historical perspective, recent controversies,” “Sedition Act of 1798: A brief history of arrests, indictments, mistreatment & abuse,” and “The First Amendment in the Colonial Newspaper Press.”

        The First Amendment Center, with offices at Vanderbilt University in Nashville, Tenn., and Washington, D.C., is an operating program of the Freedom Forum and is associated with the Newseum and the Diversity Institute. Its offices on the Vanderbilt campus are located in the John Seigenthaler Center. The center serves as a forum for the study and exploration of free-expression issues, including freedom of speech, of the press and of religion, and the rights to assemble and to petition the government.

        Library Manager/Information Specialist

        A. C. Kalmbach Memorial Library
        August 1995 – March 2003 (7 years 8 months)

        As Library Manager: Responsible for the administration and supervision of all public service and technical processing functions. Directed library reference services and collection development efforts. Prepared grant applications for special project funding. Managed library publications’ marketing and development activities. Co-edited six library publications, including historic reprints and original works. Supervised and provided direction to support staff and volunteers.

        As Information Specialist: Provided prompt reference and research support for library patrons and prepared written and oral responses to inquiries. Assisted in the development of finding aids and research tools, including computer access, physical organization, and compilation of indices, bibliographies, catalogs, and other publications. Cataloged, indexed and shelved library materials. Responsible for arrangement and description of archival materials.

        The A.C. Kalmbach Memorial Library is recognized as the "Official Railroad Library of the State of Tennessee." Its holdings include an extensive collection of railroad books, periodicals, photographs, videos and reference materials. Through a variety of publications and programs, the library promotes awareness of the crucial role that railroads played in the development of American life and culture.
