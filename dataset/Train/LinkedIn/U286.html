        Background
        Summary
        I combine my photographic skills, teaching, professional training and years of experience in both putting exhibitions together as well as working as a Public Relations professional. Over the years I've curated numerous exhibitions in UK and Greece right from scratch: I've created or taught groups and brought their work together from solo artists or group exhibitions for a variety of venues from galleries to museums, public spaces, banks, libraries etc. I've worked on numerous exhibitions from selecting, mounting, framing, hanging and presenting works of art and other related topics. 
        

        
Alongside of curating exhibitions I am a specialist in PR photography as well as in other photographic fields and in teaching photography and related topics to mainly young people and special needs groups of children and young adults. 
        
I am an expert in public relations photography, portraiture, children's portraiture and social documentary. I am particularly interested in working on long term documentary projects (see below). 
        
I will describe myself as a photography facilitator with considerable experience of teaching both the art and the technical side of photography to a wide range of people from all walks of life. I am an excellent communicator, working with young people, people with special needs, children of all ages in hospitals as well as elderly people and children of primary school age. A natural communicator with the ability to get through to people from diverse backgrounds.

        Edit
        Experience
        Curator for the School Arts Exhibition
        Leighton House Museum
        May 2012 – Present (3 years 3 months)Kensington, London
        This year 2015, the School Arts Exhibition at the Leighton House Museum will last until 28th June and is being held in the museum's Upper Perrin Gallery. The Leighton House Museum hosts every year the "School Arts Exhibition", a well established Art exhibition that has been taking place in the Museum for the last 16 years. It includes paintings, drawings, ceramics, sculptures and 3D installations from thirty three Nursery, Primary and Secondary state and public schools and a few of the colleges within the Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea. The schools prepare the art work during the school year and their best work gets submitted around the end of April. My job is to take decisions on how to present the art work and depending on the work I create the mounts, frame and present the work. 
        
I also photograph the art work which is put on the Museum's website. The final stage is the hanging arrangements of the work which in the past for a few years was shown in a Marquee in the garden of the Museum.
