        Background
        Summary
        Currently enjoying working as part of a pan-European team on the Galileo satellite navigation mission, on behalf of Airbus Defence and Space for the European Space Agency and the European Commission.
        

        
Completed the two-year Graduate Development Programme of EADS Astrium in 2010, having gained experience of technical and managerial placements in departments across the Astrium business in Portsmouth and Stevenage, to strengthen understanding of the space industry.
        

        
Graphic designer and web developer, interested in visual communication and online communities. Experienced in the design and development of standards compliant web sites and visual identities. Currently developing the website for the Parliamentary Space Committee.
        

        
Space, aeronautics and technology enthusiast, particularly interested in the application of robotics for planetary exploration. Proficient speaking and writing abilities in French.

        Galileo Systems Engineer
        Galileo Control Centre (Astrium/DLR)
        August 2011 – Present (4 years)Munich Area, Germany
        On-site at the centre of operations for the Galileo satellite navigation mission in Germany, to deploy, integrate and verify the control systems designed to operate the first satellites as they launch as part of the in-orbit validation (IOV) phase to prove the Galileo concept.
        

        
• responsibility for the central monitoring and control facility of the ground control segment to support live satellite operations;
        
• interfacing of the central facility with segment subsystems including flight dynamics, operations preparation, spacecraft control and constellation simulation;
        
• technical exchanges and meetings with the European Space Agency and subcontractors;
        
• provision of system performance analyses;
        
• liaison with subcontractors in the management and acceptance of system software deliveries, installations and upgrades;
        
• flow-down of technical requirements to subcontractors and assessment of the technical solutions provided in response;
        
• updates to the technical baseline to reflect anomaly resolution and implementation on the live system.
        

        
• chairing of test readiness reviews, execution of test campaigns and contribution to end-to-end system verification campaigns to verify compliance of the system with ESA requirements;
        
• configuration of the system applications and network infrastructure;
        
• attendance at review boards for management of anomaly reports and configuration change requests;
        
• subsequent anomaly investigation, design and implementation of resolutions;
        
• authoring and editing of test procedures and associated test data, maintenance of configuration management tools.
        

        
Gaining experience in ESA and industry-standard flight operations infrastructure, including SCOS-2000.
        

        
For more information on Galileo as a navigation project of the European Space Agency, visit: http://www.esa.int/esaNA/
