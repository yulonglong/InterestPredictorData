        Background
        Summary
        My background started in London helping to design, create, and sell intelligent homes for Grahams, a multiple award winning audio & visual home automation company.
        

        
I then left London to follow a dream of getting a Commercial pilot's license, after completion I taught myself how to build a website and helped start a company buying and selling aircraft online.
        

        
After selling that I helped start a winery called Stormhoek by helping to build our online and social media marketing with Hugh MacLeod and co. The brand got picked up as one of Ad Age’s Top 50 brands, and has been used globally in case studies as the world's first brand to successfully grow using Social Media.
        

        
I then helped launch an education company called Huddlemind with Dave Duarte. It became one of the world's first training companies to teach digital, social, and integrated marketing to global corporate executives.
        

        
I now work with WPP and Ogilvy & Mather in South Africa based in Cape Town. I work across the creative, planning, and accounts teams to help inspire / innovate / and educate in the ever evolving technology & creative space.
        
With Ogilvy I have helped launch and re-launch several of our digital divisions such as social@Ogilvy, OgilvyOne, as well as creating open agency digital initiatives such OgilvyLabs.
        

        
My job is part creative, part technologist, part planner, part education. Together with Dave Duarte I co-founded the Ogilvy Digital Marketing Academy. This course helps train Ogilvy clients and agency staff on the digital evolution of advertising, and helps to shift and evolve agency culture.
        

        
With WPP I am the Director of Stream Africa, a yearly unconference that brings together thought leaders in the tech, art, and entrepreneurial space with leaders from WPP clients and agencies.
        

        
I also help out with various South African digital marketing industry boards/events, as well as being a mentor for some of Microsoft's and Google’s start-up projects in Africa.
